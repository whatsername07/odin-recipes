Chicken Katsu Curry

Chicken katsu curry is a popular Japanese dish that features a crispy, deep-fried chicken cutlet, known as "katsu," served alongside a thick, savory-sweet Japanese curry sauce and often steamed rice. The chicken is typically coated in panko breadcrumbs, which are large and flaky, giving it a uniquely crunchy texture. The curry sauce is distinct from South Asian curries, being generally mild, rich, and slightly sweet with aromatic, sometimes fruity notes, achieved through ingredients like grated apple or honey, along with a blend of spices. The contrast between the crunchy coating of the chicken and the smooth, velvety sauce is what makes this dish a satisfying and much-loved favorite.

Ingredients

  • cooking oil
  • 2 onions
  • 2 large carrots
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • thumb-sized piece of ginger
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 400ml can coconut milk
  • 2 tsp honey
  • Breaded chicken fillet
  • Fresh boiled rice

Method

  1. Heat 1 tbsp of oil in the pan, and cook the onions and carrots till the onions are soft (~8mins).
  2. Add the garlic and ginger and sizzle for about 30 seconds, then add in the curry powder and turmeric.
  3. Add the coconut milk and honey, season well, and cover and simmer over a low heat for 20 mins. Optionally, blend it for a smoother texture.
  4. Cook your fillet and boil your rice (making sure to wash it beforehand!), and assemble the rice, chicken and sauce to serve.
