Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish, and one of the oldest forms of pasta, consisting of wide, flat pasta sheets layered with various ingredients and baked in the oven. The most common preparation, often called Lasagna al Forno (baked lasagna), involves alternating layers of lasagna pasta, a rich ragù (a meat-based sauce, typically beef or a combination of meats), and a creamy béchamel sauce or ricotta cheese, all generously topped with cheese. The result is a hearty, deeply flavored casserole with distinct layers of soft pasta, savory meat sauce, and melted, bubbly cheese, making it a beloved comfort food worldwide.

  • 900g beef Mince
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 onions, roughly chopped
  • 4 sticks celery
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 150 ml beef stock
  • 3 tbsp tomato puree
  • 1 tbsp chopped thyme
  • 2x 400g tins chopped tomatoes
  • 50g butter
  • 50g plain flour
  • 750ml hot milk
  • 50g parmessn
  • 2tsp dijon mustard
  • salt and pepper (freshly ground)
  • 12 lasagna sheets
  • 75g mature cheddar, grated

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 160C/140C fan.
  2. Heat a large frying pan til hot and add the oil. Then brown the mince and transfer to a plate when done.
  3. Add the onion, celery and garlic to the pan and cook till softened. Return meat to the pan and stir the flour in. Add the stock and bring to boil, then add tomato puree and thyme and stir well.
  4. Stir in the tinned tomatoes, bring to boil again, cover and simmer in the oven for 1-1.5 hours, or till the beef is tender.
  5. In a separate pan, melt the butter. Then add the flour and cook over the heat for a minute. Gradually whisk in the hot milk till the sauce is thickened. Add the mustard and parmesan, and season generously with salt and pepper.
  6. Layer the lasagna in alternating layers of ragu, white sauce and pasta until all are used up, then top with cheddar and cook in the oven for 45 minutes.
